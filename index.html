Getting Started

Last updated on 1 April 2026

Install OpsPilot, take a quick tour, and create your first note, task, and workflow in minutes.

Installing OpsPilot

Go to the Shopify App Store and search for OpsPilot
Click Add app
Review the permissions OpsPilot needs (read orders, products, customers, inventory, checkouts)
Click Install app
You'll be redirected to the OpsPilot dashboard inside your Shopify Admin
💡
OpsPilot runs inside your Shopify Admin — there's no separate website to log in to. Just click the app from your Shopify sidebar.

First-Time Setup

When you install OpsPilot for the first time, the app automatically:

  • Creates your user account (using your Shopify staff email)
  • Sets up default task statuses (Pending, In Progress, Completed, etc.)
  • Detects your store timezone and currency
  • Prepares sample workflow templates to get you started

Notes

Last updated on 1 April 2026

Add comments, instructions, and context to your Shopify orders, products, and customers. Like sticky notes for your store data.

What Are Notes?

Notes let you add comments, instructions, and context to your Shopify data that your whole team can see. Think of them as sticky notes attached to your store records.

  • "Customer requested gift wrapping" → attached to an Order
  • "Restock by Friday, supplier: ABC Inc." → attached to a Product
  • "VIP customer, always offer free shipping" → attached to a Customer
  • "Weekly team meeting agenda" → standalone note (not attached to anything)

Creating a Note

Click Notes in the sidebar
Click the Create Note button (top right)
Choose an Entity Type: Order, Product, Customer, or None (standalone)
If you chose an entity type, select a specific record from the dropdown

Click Save

Workflows

Last updated on 1 April 2026

Automate repetitive operations — when something happens in your store, OpsPilot can create tasks, send emails, post to Slack, and more.

What Are Workflows?

Workflows let you automate repetitive operations. When something happens (a new order, low inventory, abandoned checkout), OpsPilot can automatically create tasks, send emails, post to Slack, log to Google Sheets — or any combination.

💡
Example: "When a new order over $500 is placed, create a task for the fulfillment team and send a Slack alert."

Creating a Workflow

Click Workflows in the sidebar
Click New Workflow
Give it a name (e.g., "High-Value Order Alert")
Choose a trigger — the event that starts the workflow
Add conditions (optional) — filters to narrow when it runs
Add actions — what should happen when the trigger fires
Click Save, then toggle the workflow Active

Triggers

A trigger is the event that starts your workflow. You can choose one trigger per workflow.

Event-Based Triggers

These fire instantly when something happens in your Shopify store:

TriggerFires When…Example Use
Order CreatedA new order is placedAlert team for high-value orders
Order UpdatedAn order changesTrack payment or fulfillment changes
Checkout AbandonedCustomer leaves checkoutSend recovery reminder
Inventory Level UpdatedStock quantity changesAlert when running low
Customer CreatedNew customer accountWelcome email, VIP tagging
Product UpdatedA product is modifiedReview task for quality control
Manual TriggerYou click "Run Now"On-demand escalations

Scheduled Trigger

Run workflows on a set schedule (daily, weekly, monthly, or custom interval). Perfect for batch operations like daily unfulfilled orders checks.

Conditions (Optional)

Conditions let you filter when the workflow runs. Without conditions, the workflow triggers on every matching event.

Operators

OperatorMeaningExample
EqualsExact matchfinancial_status equals "pending"
Not EqualDoes not matchfulfillment_status not equal "fulfilled"
Greater ThanNumeric comparisontotal_price greater than 500
Less ThanNumeric comparisonavailable less than 10
ContainsText includes valuetags contains "vip"
ExistsField is presentemail exists

AND / OR Groups

Combine conditions with AND (all must be true) or OR (at least one). You can nest groups for complex logic:

IF (order.total_price > 500 AND order.tags contains "wholesale")
   OR (order.total_price > 1000)
→ Run actions

Actions

Actions are what happens when the trigger fires and conditions are met. You can add multiple actions — they run in order.

Create Task

Automatically creates a task for your team. Set the title, description, priority, assignee, and due date offset.

📝
Example: Title: Review high-value order #{{order_number}} — Priority: High — Assign To: John — Due: 2 days

Create Note

Automatically creates an internal note. Set the title, content, entity type, and whether to pin it.

Send Email

Send an email to a customer, team member, or custom address. Supports HTML and template variables.

⚠️
Requires email integration to be configured. Not available on the Free plan.

Send Slack Message

Posts a message to your Slack channel. Supports Slack markdown and template variables.

⚠️
Requires Slack integration to be configured. Not available on the Free plan.

Add to Google Sheet

Appends a row to a Google Sheets spreadsheet. Great for reports, audit trails, and dashboards. Supports deduplication and custom styling.

Template Variables

Template variables let you insert dynamic data from the trigger into your actions. Wrap them in double curly braces: {{variable_name}}.

Delayed Actions

Add a delay to any action so it runs later instead of immediately. Enable Delay in the action settings and set the duration.

DelayUse Case

2 hoursRemind fulfillment team if order still pending

Deduplication (Smart Repeat Prevention)

OpsPilot automatically prevents duplicates:

  • Task Deduplication: When "Create Once Per Entity" is enabled (default), existing tasks for the same entity are updated instead of creating duplicates
  • Email / Slack / Sheets: Identical notifications are not sent twice within 24 hours by default

Slack Integration

Post automated messages to your Slack workspace from workflows.

Option A: Bot Token (Recommended)

Create a Slack App at api.slack.com/apps
Under OAuth & Permissions, add scopes: chat:write and chat:write.public
Install the app to your workspace and copy the Bot User OAuth Token
In OpsPilot: Settings → Integrations → Slack, paste the token
Set a Default Channel and click Verify

Option B: Webhook URL (Simpler)

A Webhook URL is easier but only posts to one channel. Create an incoming webhook at Slack, paste the URL in OpsPilot, and verify.

📸
Screenshot: Slack integration setup — Bot Token and channel configuration

Google Sheets Integration

Log data from workflows directly into Google Sheets — great for reports and audit trails.

Go to Google Cloud Console and create a project

Go to IAM & Admin → Service Accounts and create one
Create a JSON key and download it

Step 2: Share Your Spreadsheet

Open your Google Sheet, click Share, and add the service account email (from the JSON file) as an Editor.

Step 3: Add to OpsPilot

Go to Settings → Integrations → Google Sheets
Upload the JSON key file or paste the credentials
Click Verify to test the connection

FAQ & Troubleshooting

Last updated on 1 April 2026

Common questions, known issues, and how to get help.

General Questions

What is OpsPilot?

OpsPilot is a Shopify app that helps you manage store operations. It provides Notes to annotate orders, products, and customers; Tasks to track what needs to be done; and Workflows to automate repetitive actions.

Does OpsPilot work with all Shopify plans?

Yes. OpsPilot works with all Shopify plans (Basic, Shopify, Advanced, Plus).

Is my data secure?

Yes. OpsPilot uses encrypted connections, stores sensitive data in encrypted form, and follows security best practices. Your Shopify data is accessed through Shopify's official API with the permissions you approve.

Notes FAQ

How many notes can I create?

Unlimited on all plans (including Free).

Can I attach files to notes?

Yes. You can upload images, PDFs, and documents. See the Notes guide for details.

Can I convert a note to a task?

Yes. Select notes and click Convert to Tasks. Each note becomes a task and they remain linked.

I deleted a note by mistake. Can I recover it?

Notes move to a soft-deleted state and can be restored. Check the note list for a "Deleted" filter.

Tasks FAQ

How do I change a task's status?

  • Table View: Click the status badge on the task row
  • Kanban View: Drag the task card to a different column
  • Detail View: Open the task → Edit → change the status

Can I create custom task statuses?

Yes. Go to Settings → Task Statuses and click Add Status.

What's the difference between Table and Kanban view?

Table shows tasks in a spreadsheet-style list — good for sorting. Kanban shows cards on a board organized by status — good for visual tracking and drag-and-drop.

Workflows FAQ

My workflow isn't triggering. Why?

  1. Workflow is not active — Make sure the toggle is set to Active
  2. Conditions are too restrictive — Temporarily remove conditions to test
  3. Wrong trigger type — Verify the trigger matches the event you expect
  4. Plan limit reached — Check if you've hit your monthly run limit
  5. Paused due to failures — Check for a "Paused Due to Failure" badge

My workflow shows "Paused Due to Failure." How do I fix it?

This means the workflow failed 4 times in a row. Check the Execution History for error messages, fix the root cause, then click Re-enable.

Can I test a workflow without waiting for a real event?

Yes. Open the workflow and click Test Workflow. Pick a real order, product, or customer as test data. Test runs count toward your monthly quota.

What are template variables?

Template variables let you insert dynamic data into actions. For example, {{order_number}} inserts the actual order number. See the Workflows guide for all variables.

Emails from my workflow aren't being sent. Why?

  1. Email integration not configured — Set up your domain in Settings
  2. Domain not verified — DNS records might not have propagated yet
  3. On Free plan — Email actions require Starter or Growth
  4. Deduplication — Same email may have been sent in the last 24 hours

Billing FAQ

Can I try a paid plan before committing?

Yes. New users get a 15-day free trial of Starter or Growth. No charge until the trial ends.

What happens if I exceed my monthly run limit?

Workflows stop executing for the rest of the month. You can upgrade your plan or wait for the next billing cycle.

Can I cancel anytime?

Yes. Downgrade to the Free plan at any time from the Billing page. The change takes effect at the end of your current billing cycle.
